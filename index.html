Temperature Matters

Butter should be at cool room temperature (about 65Â°F). Test by pressing with your finger - it should give slightly but not be greasy or melted.

Chill Your Dough

Chilling cookie dough for at least 30 minutes (or up to 72 hours) develops the flavor and prevents excessive spreading during baking.

Consistent Sizing

Use a cookie scoop for uniform cookie sizes. This ensures even baking and professional-looking results every time.

The Dough Texture Test

Properly mixed cookie dough should hold together when squeezed but shouldn't be sticky. If it's too dry, add milk 1 tsp at a time. If too sticky, add flour 1 tbsp at a time.

For chocolate chip cookies, the dough should be soft but not wet. When you press it flat with your fingers, the edges should crack slightly but maintain their shape.
